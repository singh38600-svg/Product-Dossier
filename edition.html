Edition 001 · Delta Exchange

Building trust at the exact moment traders lose certainty.

An independent investigation into Delta Exchange’s automation stack, public product story, user trust signals and product opportunities.

Published16 July 2026
Research modeDeep public-source review
AudienceCompany and product leadership
StatusVersion 1.0

Working thesis

Delta has assembled an unusually connected retail-to-pro automation stack. Its strongest next advantage may be making every order, fee, balance movement and automated strategy explainable under stress.

Executive snapshot

The evidence suggests that Delta’s most distinctive public asset is not one isolated feature. It is the path from native trading to TradingView, APIs, AI-assisted code generation and a strategy marketplace. The opportunity is to make that path safer and easier to trust.

Differentiator

Automation journey

APIs, TradingView, Trading Bot, API Copilot and Algo Marketplace form a connected stack.

Trust gap

Event explainability

Users need a shared, authoritative account of orders, triggers, fills, fees and balance changes.

Immediate fix

Product truth

Synchronize public descriptions of spot, custody, availability and risk across official surfaces.

Research limit

Internal data

Retention, market quality, incident prevalence and support volumes cannot be inferred reliably from public evidence.

What surprised us

The strongest findings appeared at the intersections—where product documentation, legal language, user signals and competitor strategy did not line up neatly.

Finding 01

The automation stack is stronger than the public narrative.

Official materials describe APIs, TradingView integration, bot tooling, AI-assisted API development and a phased Algo Marketplace.

Evidence: E-003, E-004, E-005, E-009 High confidence
Finding 02

Trust breaks when users cannot reconstruct events.

Visible user signals concentrate on chart state, order confidence, fees, support and withdrawal timing. These are discovery signals, not population estimates.

Evidence: E-006, E-007, E-008 Medium confidence
Finding 03

Public product truth is not fully synchronized.

Current official surfaces use different language for spot availability, INR holdings, digital assets and custody arrangements.

Evidence: E-001, E-002 High confidence

Flagship proposal: Trade Integrity Center

When a user disputes a stop-loss, liquidation, fill or balance change, reassurance is not enough. The product should help the user reconstruct the event from authoritative system records.

Help me prove what happened—not merely accept that the system was right.
01 · SubmittedOrder payload, client timestamp, acknowledgement and validation result.
02 · TriggeredMark price, index price, trigger rule and data freshness.
03 · MatchedQueue status, partial fills, execution price, slippage and rejection.
04 · ReconciledP&L, fees, GST, funding, liquidation and balance change.
05 · ExportedPortable record for support, grievance or personal audit.

What we would test first

These are experiments, not prescriptions. Each is designed to reduce a specific uncertainty before a large investment is made.

Experiment

Order timeline MVP

Expose server-authoritative state for selected order types to a small cohort and measure dispute resolution.

Primary outcome
−30%

Directional target for trade-dispute contacts per active trader—not a forecast.

Guardrail

No false certainty

Every displayed event must originate from authoritative logs. Missing data must remain visible.
