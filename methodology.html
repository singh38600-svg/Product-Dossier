Methodology

Designed to make confident mistakes harder.

The process combines systematic searching, product analysis, competitive intelligence, structured hypothesis testing and explicit uncertainty.

The research loop

Each cycle identifies gaps, gathers additional evidence, updates the ledger, tests alternatives and records what would change the conclusion.

01 · Frame

Define the decision, audience, scope, constraints and falsifiable questions.

02 · Discover

Search official, regulatory, product, competitor and user evidence.

03 · Evaluate

Assess relevance, directness, transparency, independence and currency.

04 · Challenge

Search for contradictions, alternatives and evidence that would disprove the thesis.

05 · Publish

Expose evidence, limitations, uncertainty, rejected claims and update triggers.

Confidence language

Confidence describes the strength of the evidence and reasoning—not how strongly the author feels about the conclusion.

High confidence

Direct and corroborated

Multiple relevant sources with few plausible competing explanations.

Medium confidence

Useful but incomplete

Reasonable support exists, but important gaps or alternatives remain.

Low confidence

A hypothesis to test

Limited direct evidence; included because the potential decision value is high.

Source hierarchy

Prestige is not proof. Sources are evaluated in relation to the claim they are being used to support.

Primary legal and regulatory material

Laws, government notifications, filings and official terms.

Direct product evidence

Official documentation, product surfaces, fee schedules, app listings and observed journeys.

Transparent datasets and rigorous research

Sources with inspectable methods, definitions and limitations.

Credible journalism and expert analysis

Used for context, investigation leads and claims that can be traced further.

User-generated evidence

Reviews and public discussions identify possible problems; they do not establish prevalence.

Quality-control questions

Before publication, each major conclusion is pressure-tested against a fixed set of questions.

Evidence integrity

Can the claim be traced? Is the source current? Does it support the exact wording? Is it independent or merely repeated?

Alternative explanations

Could rollout differences, stale documentation, user misunderstanding, selection bias or another cause explain the observation?

Decision usefulness

Would this finding help a leader choose what to investigate, test, stop or prioritize?

Intellectual honesty

Are unknowns visible? Are severe allegations treated as allegations? Are internal-data gaps left unfilled?
